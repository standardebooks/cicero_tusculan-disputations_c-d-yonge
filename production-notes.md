(1) The original book used roman numerals to number many paragraphs in each chapter; I have removed those numbers.

(2) I've also made some changes to punctuation; the original translator was in the habit of stringing many sentences together with colons and semicolons and dashes in a way that harmed readability.

(3) In chapter 2, there is an error in the original, where the paragraph beginning "Let the Stoics, then..." and those that follow is attributed to Cicero's interrogator and not to Cicero himself; I have corrected this.

(4) The original was en-GB; the PG transcribers partially converted it to en-US, and I have continued their work in that direction whereever I noticed it to be incomplete.

(5) I have added a style for paragraphs within table cells so as to distinguish genuinely new paragraphs from paragraphs that are only there for markup-purposes (as there isn't a convenient way to embed a `<blockquote>` within a paragraph).
